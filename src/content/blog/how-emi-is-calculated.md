Your **EMI** (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed amount you pay each month until a loan is repaid. Knowing how it's calculated helps you compare loans honestly.

## The formula

EMI uses the reducing-balance formula:

> EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ ÷ ( (1 + r)ⁿ − 1 )

where **P** is the principal, **r** is the monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and **n** is the number of monthly instalments.

## Flat vs reducing balance — the trap

A **reducing-balance** loan charges interest only on what you still owe, which falls every month. A **flat-rate** loan charges interest on the full original amount for the whole term — so a "flat 8%" is far more expensive than a "reducing 8%". Always compare loans on reducing balance.

## Tenure changes everything

- A **longer tenure** lowers the monthly EMI but raises the **total interest** a lot.
- A **lower rate** cuts both EMI and total interest.
- **Prepayments** reduce the outstanding principal, removing future interest.

Try moving the tenure slider in the [EMI calculator](/calculators/emi) and watch the *total interest* figure — it's usually the most eye-opening number, and it's where the real cost of a loan hides.
