If you've ever needed to merge two PDFs or rotate a scanned page, you've probably hit a wall of sites that demand an email, slap a watermark on your file, or quietly upload your documents to a server you don't control. SnapJar takes a different approach: every PDF tool below runs 100% in your browser, so your files never leave your device. Here are seven free PDF tools you can use right now, with no upload, no watermark, and no signup.

## 1. Merge PDF

Combining multiple PDFs into one file is the most common PDF task, and you shouldn't have to upload sensitive documents to do it. The [Merge PDF](/tools/pdf-tools) tool lets you select several PDFs, reorder them, and stitch them into a single document directly in the browser. Because the merging happens locally, even large multi-file contracts or reports stay private. The output is a clean, watermark-free PDF you can download instantly.

## 2. Split PDF

Sometimes you only need a few pages out of a long document, like a single section of a lease or one chapter of a manual. The [Split PDF](/tools/split-pdf) tool lets you extract specific pages or break a large file into smaller pieces without any software install. You pick the page ranges, and the tool generates new PDFs on the spot. This is ideal for sharing just the relevant part of a document instead of the whole thing.

## 3. Rotate PDF

Scanned pages and phone-captured documents often end up sideways or upside down. The [Rotate PDF](/tools/rotate-pdf) tool fixes orientation by rotating individual pages or the entire file in 90-degree increments. Once you've corrected the layout, you download a properly oriented PDF that opens the right way every time. It's a small fix that makes a document far easier to read and print.

## 4. Add Page Numbers

Long documents are much easier to navigate and reference when every page is numbered. The [Add Page Numbers](/tools/add-page-numbers) tool overlays clean page numbers onto your PDF, which is especially handy for reports, manuscripts, and legal filings that require pagination. You can apply numbering across the whole document and download the result without any branding or watermark. Everything is processed on your machine, so confidential drafts stay confidential.

## 5. Images to PDF (JPG to PDF)

Turning a stack of photos or screenshots into a single shareable document is a frequent need, whether you're submitting receipts or assembling a portfolio. The [JPG to PDF](/tools/jpg-to-pdf) tool combines your images into one PDF, preserving the order you choose. Because it runs in the browser, you can convert personal photos or ID scans without sending them to a third party. The output is a standard PDF that works in any reader.

## 6. PDF to JPG

The reverse is just as useful: extracting images from a PDF so you can drop a page into a presentation, email, or social post. The [PDF to JPG](/tools/pdf-to-jpg) tool converts each PDF page into an image you can save individually. This is perfect when someone needs a quick preview of a document but can't open a PDF, or when you want to reuse a chart or diagram. As with every tool here, the conversion happens locally with nothing uploaded.

## 7. Compress and Watermark (Bonus Tools)

PDFs and the images inside them can get heavy, making them hard to email or upload to forms with size limits. While SnapJar's [Image Compressor](/tools/compress) is built for images, it pairs naturally with the PDF workflow when you compress photos before turning them into a PDF, keeping the final file lean. And if you actually want to brand a document on your own terms, the [Watermark](/tools/watermark) tool lets you add your own text or logo, the opposite of the forced watermarks other sites impose. Both run entirely in your browser, so you stay in control of every file.
