A watermark keeps your photos yours. Whether you're a photographer protecting a portfolio or a brand keeping its logo visible, here's how to add one in seconds — without uploading your images anywhere.

## Why watermark at all?

A visible mark deters casual re-use, keeps your brand attached to an image wherever it travels, and makes theft obvious. The most theft-resistant option is a **tiled** watermark repeated across the whole photo; the cleanest for portfolios is a small mark in a corner.

## Step by step

1. Open the [Watermark Studio](/tools/watermark) and drop in one or many photos (batch mode shows them in a strip up top).
2. Pick a type: **Text**, **Logo** or **Signature**.
3. Style it — font, colour, opacity, rotation and position. Turn on **Tile** to repeat it across the image.
4. Click **Download** for the current photo, or **Download all** for the whole batch.

## Tips for a good watermark

- **Opacity 40–70%** is usually right — visible but not ruining the photo.
- A **drop shadow** keeps a light watermark readable on bright areas.
- For brand work, a **logo** at low opacity in a corner looks professional; for proofs, **tile** your name across everything.

Everything runs locally in your browser, so even private images never leave your device.
