Money decisions get easier when you can see the numbers before you commit. Whether you're sizing up a loan, planning a monthly investment, or estimating your tax bill, a good calculator turns guesswork into a clear answer in seconds. Here are seven free financial calculators worth bookmarking — every one runs entirely in your browser on SnapJar, so nothing you type is ever uploaded.

## 1. EMI Calculator

Before you sign a loan agreement, you want to know exactly what your monthly payment will be. The [EMI Calculator](/calculators/emi) takes your loan amount, interest rate, and tenure and returns your equated monthly installment along with the total interest you'll pay over the life of the loan. This makes it easy to compare a shorter tenure with higher payments against a longer one with lower payments. Use it for personal, car, or any fixed-rate loan to avoid surprises down the road.

## 2. Mortgage Calculator

A home loan is usually the biggest commitment most people make, so the math deserves a closer look. The [Mortgage Calculator](/calculators/mortgage) estimates your monthly home loan payment from the principal, interest rate, and loan term, and shows how much of your money goes toward interest versus the property itself. Seeing the full repayment picture helps you decide on a down payment size or whether to negotiate a better rate. Even a small rate difference can mean a large change in what you ultimately pay.

## 3. SIP Calculator

Systematic Investment Plans let you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals, and the [SIP Calculator](/calculators/sip) shows how those contributions can grow over time. Enter your monthly investment, an expected annual return, and the number of years to see your projected corpus and how much of it comes from your own contributions versus growth. This is one of the clearest ways to understand the power of investing consistently rather than waiting to invest a lump sum. It's a great tool for setting realistic long-term goals.

## 4. Compound Interest Calculator

Compound interest is the engine behind almost every long-term savings plan, and seeing it in action is genuinely motivating. The [Compound Interest Calculator](/calculators/compound-interest) shows how an initial amount grows when returns are reinvested over time, factoring in your principal, rate, and compounding frequency. The difference between simple and compound growth becomes obvious once you stretch the timeline out over years or decades. Use it to understand why starting early matters far more than starting big.

## 5. Fixed Deposit Calculator

If you prefer predictable, low-risk returns, a fixed deposit is a popular choice — and the [FD Calculator](/calculators/fd) tells you exactly what you'll earn. Plug in your deposit amount, interest rate, and tenure to see your maturity value and the total interest accrued. Because FD returns are fixed at the time you invest, the calculator gives you a reliable number you can plan around. It's handy for comparing offers from different banks or deciding between tenures.

## 6. Income Tax Calculator

Tax planning is far less stressful when you can estimate your liability ahead of filing season. The [Income Tax Calculator](/calculators/income-tax) helps you work out your approximate tax based on your income and applicable slabs, so you can budget for what you owe or plan deductions in advance. Knowing the number early gives you time to make smart, legitimate moves before the year closes. It's a useful sanity check whether you're salaried or have additional income to account for.

## 7. Retirement Calculator

Retirement feels distant until you put real numbers to it, and that's where the [Retirement Calculator](/calculators/retirement) earns its place on your bookmark bar. It projects how much you might accumulate by your target retirement age based on your current savings, ongoing contributions, and expected returns. Seeing the gap between where you are and where you want to be is the first step toward closing it. Adjust the inputs to test different saving rates and find a plan that feels achievable.
