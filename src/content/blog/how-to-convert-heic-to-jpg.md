Opened an iPhone photo on your PC and got an error? That's **HEIC** — Apple's space-saving format. It's great on Apple devices but often won't open on Windows, older Androids, or many websites. The fix is to convert it to **JPG**.

## What is HEIC?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) stores photos at roughly half the size of JPG at similar quality. iPhones use it by default. The trade-off is compatibility: lots of apps and sites still can't read it.

## Convert it in your browser

1. Open the [HEIC to JPG converter](/tools/heic-to-jpg).
2. Drop in one or many `.heic` files (batch is supported).
3. Choose a quality and let it convert.
4. Download each JPG, or all of them at once.

No software to install, and your photos are **never uploaded** — the conversion happens entirely on your device.

## Want to stop HEIC at the source?

On your iPhone, go to **Settings → Camera → Formats** and choose **Most Compatible** to shoot in JPG instead. But for the photos you already have, a quick browser conversion is the easiest fix.
